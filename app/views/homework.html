  1. Read the following article http://martinfowler.com/articles/microservices.html and explain what "Microservices Architecture" means.

    It's a way to structure a team or a working flow to focus on making each part of the system a subsystem itself, with the aim of making it as independent as possible so that it may be upgraded or replaced in the future without any, or as few as possible, modifications to the rest of the system. It also involves changing the way the teams in a company are structures since instead of dividing the workers by skills (developers, designers, managers), they are divided into tinier teams composing of a balance between all of them so that they may develop and maintain the microservice they work on. This can be clearly seen with Amazon, specifically with their AWS products, since they completely separated the services they used on their webpage and made each service a product itself.

  4. Explain how routes work in angular and a code example.

    Routes in angular work through a service ($route) that links URLs to views and controllers. You interact with it through the $routeProvider module, where you define the route it leads to and the route object with the mapping information that will be used to resolve that URL. In this route object you define the controller (which controls its behaviour), and the view (which defines how it looks), among other things.
